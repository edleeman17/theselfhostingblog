Forget needing to add your ssh and wpa_supplicant files! Here's a way to do it from the Raspberry Pi Imager

Happy Monday! Here's your weekly dose of self-hosting goodies!

First up, let me tell you about a huge time saver that I have recently found out about. We all know and love the Raspberry Pi Imager.

We can quickly download a multitude of images direct from the application, without having to dig through the internet for the latest version of Ubuntu Server.

It's pretty awesome. Now, what's this time saver?

Well, you can now forget having to eject and re-insert your SD Card just to add your .ssh and wpa_supplicant.conf files to get set up. You can do this all from the Raspberry Pi Imager application!

But how? There's no interface other than the three main CTA buttons?!

CTRL + SHIFT + X - There we go, the secret is out.

As you can see from the above demo, you can configure your host name, enable SSH, specify your SSH Key or provide a custom password for the PI user and even configure your WIFI SSID and Password. All from the Raspberry Pi Imager!

Hopefully you'll find this as useful as I have!

Have a great week!

Ed
