<h6 id="%F0%9F%93%B0-the-self-hosting-blog-%E2%80%A2-issue-12">📰 The Self-Hosting Blog • Issue #12</h6><p>Happy Monday! Here's your weekly dose of self-hosting goodies!</p><p>First up, let me tell you about a huge time saver that I have recently found out about. We all know and love the Raspberry Pi Imager. </p><p>We can quickly download a multitude of images direct from the application, without having to dig through the internet for the latest version of Ubuntu Server.</p><figure class="kg-card kg-image-card"><img src="/images/2021/05/Peek-2021-05-02-20-24.gif" class="kg-image" alt loading="lazy" width="682" height="422"></figure><p>It's pretty awesome. Now, what's this time saver?</p><p>Well, you can now forget having to eject and re-insert your SD Card just to add your <code>.ssh</code> and <code>wpa_supplicant.conf</code> files to get set up. You can do this all from the Raspberry Pi Imager application!</p><p>But how? There's no interface other than the three main CTA buttons?!</p><p><code>CTRL + SHIFT + X</code> - There we go, the secret is out.</p><figure class="kg-card kg-image-card"><img src="/images/2021/05/Peek-2021-05-02-20-29.gif" class="kg-image" alt loading="lazy" width="679" height="418"></figure><p>As you can see from the above demo, you can configure your host name, enable SSH, specify your SSH Key or provide a custom password for the PI user and even configure your WIFI SSID and Password.
