<h6 id="the-self-hosting-blog-%E2%80%A2-issue-8">The Self-Hosting Blog • Issue #8</h6><p>Happy Monday! Hope you had a good weekend!</p><p>I'm going to talk to you about adding your YouTube subscriptions as RSS feeds. You may have noticed while viewing your favourite YouTube content creators, that they ask you to "subscribe and click the bell icon" to be notified when they upload. </p><p>This is because YouTube is in control of whether their content gets shown to you. Have you ever missed an upload because you didn't know that it existed?</p><p>RSS is here to help. I'm going to be writing a post in the future around RSS and why I and others use it daily. The long and short of it is, RSS is a direct feed on content that you want to see, without fancy algorithms or advertisements (depending on your RSS reader.). </p><p>You can linearly see all your news, rather than what a computer decides for you.</p><p>Unfortunately, YouTube has disabled the really useful feature of exporting all of your YouTube subscriptions to an OPML file. So we are going to have to take the long route.</p><p>The manual way to do this is to navigate to the channel of the YouTube creator you'd like to follow. In the URL you should see a channel ID.<br>For example.</p><pre><code>https://www.youtube.com/channel/UCOk-gHyjcWZNj3Br4oxwh0A</code></pre><p>Here you will find the string <code>UCOk-gHyjcWZNj3Br4oxwh0A</code> this is the channel ID.</p><p>Now, you just need to concatenate that channel ID to the following string.</p><pre><code>https://www.youtube.com/feeds/videos.xml?channel_id=</code></pre><p>You can now paste this URL into your favourite RSS feed.</p><p>Here's the link to TechnoTim's RSS feed that has just been generated.</p><figure class="kg-card kg-bookmark-card"><a class="kg-bookmark-container" href="https://www.youtube.com/feeds/videos.xml?channel_id&#x3D;UCOk-gHyjcWZNj3Br4oxwh0A"><div class="kg-bookmark-content"><div class="kg-bookmark-title">Techno Tim</div><div class="kg-bookmark-description"></div><div class="kg-bookmark-metadata"><span class="kg-bookmark-author">Which one should I choose?What is a HomeLab and How Do I Get Started?Windows Terminal and WSL Config Fast, Simple, and Easy GuideHow to Update Portainer Fast, Simple, and Easy GuideContainerizing HandBrake with Docker and KubernetesHow to Create a Multiboot USB with Ventoy Fast, Simple, and Easy GuideThe Best Way to Dual Boot Windows and UbuntuI ran MY LIFE through a DEEP LEARNING algorithm and here&#x27;s what came out...RTX 3090 for Machine Learning and Object Detection?My HUGE Home Security UpgradeSelf-host your own internet speed test with LibreSpeed!Cloud Native Distributed Storage in Kubernetes with LonghornAutomate EVERYTHING with Ansible!</span></div></div></a></figure><p>By doing this, you'll be able to absorb all of your favourite content without worrying about missing anything.</p><p>You could also use this to de-google yourself as you no longer need to have a YouTube account to follow your 'subscriptions'.</p><p>A less manual method could be to use the following Firefox extension. This not only applies to YouTube but any website on the internet.</p><figure class="kg-card kg-bookmark-card"><a class="kg-bookmark-container" href="https://addons.mozilla.org/en-US/firefox/addon/rsspreview/"><div class="kg-bookmark-content"><div class="kg-bookmark-title">RSSPreview – Get this Extension for 🦊 Firefox (en-US)</div><div class="kg-bookmark-description">Preview RSS feeds in-browser</div><div class="kg-bookmark-metadata"><img class="kg-bookmark-icon" src="https://addons.cdn.mozilla.net/favicon.ico?v&#x3D;2"><span class="kg-bookmark-publisher">Aurelien David</span></div></div><div class="kg-bookmark-thumbnail"><img src="https://addons.cdn.mozilla.net/user-media/previews/full/210/210319.png?modified&#x3D;1616527404"></div></a></figure><p>RSSPreview will scan the page that you're currently viewing to determine whether there is an RSS feed that can be followed.</p><p>Or, you can use <a href="https://rss.yt/">https://rss.yt/</a> which will allow you to paste in the YouTube channel URL and form an RSS feed URL for you.</p><p>Anyway, there's a little something for you, sorry about not posting too much recently.</p><p>Hope you had an awesome Easter weekend!</p><p>Ed</p><hr><figure class="kg-card kg-image-card"><img src="/images/2021/03/photo-1484788032193-83c907055f21.jpg" class="kg-image" alt loading="lazy" width="2000" height="1333" srcset="/images/size/w600/2021/03/photo-1484788032193-83c907055f21.jpg 600w, /images/size/w1000/2021/03/photo-1484788032193-83c907055f21.jpg 1000w, /images/size/w1600/2021/03/photo-1484788032193-83c907055f21.jpg 1600w, /images/2021/03/photo-1484788032193-83c907055f21.jpg 2000w" sizes="(min-width: 720px) 720px"></figure><h1 id="self-hosting-papercupsioopen-source-live-customer-chat-with-slack-integration">Self-hosting Papercups.io -
